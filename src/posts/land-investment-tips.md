# Top 5 Land Investment Tips in Erode

Investing in vacant land is one of the most reliable ways to build long-term generational wealth. However, without meticulous due diligence, land buyers can face severe legal disputes or purchase illiquid plots. Here are the top 5 checkpoints compiled by our senior land legal advisors.

## 1. Trace the Title History for at least 30 Years

Ensure the seller holds a clear, unencumbered title. A parent document trace going back 30 years is required to confirm that the chain of ownership is completely unbroken.
*   **Encumbrance Certificate (EC):** Obtain a fresh EC from the Sub-Registrar's office for the past 30 years to verify that no active mortgages or liens exist against the property.

## 2. Check for DTCP Approval and RERA Registration

Local bodies (Panchayats) frequently sell plots without authority to approve layouts. Ensure your layout has a valid **DTCP approval number** and, if it contains more than 8 plots, a **RERA registry number**.
*   Buying unapproved plots means you cannot get building permits or bank loan approvals.

## 3. Verify Physical Access Roads

Ensure that the road leading to your plot is officially marked in the government revenue map. Many developers create temporary private roads that are later blocked by surrounding land owners.
*   The minimum width for residential approach roads is **30 feet** for layout approvals.

## 4. Check Zonal Classification

Ensure the land is classified under a residential zone in the city's master plan. Agricultural land, wetland, or industrial-classified zones cannot be used for housing without obtaining a formal Land Use Conversion certificate.

## 5. Verify Boundary Stones and FSI (Floor Space Index)

Request a government surveyor to measure the property and mark boundary lines before registration. Also, check the FSI limits for the area, which determines the maximum built-up square footage allowed.
